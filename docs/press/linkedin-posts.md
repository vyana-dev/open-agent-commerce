## Post A — invite collaborators / builders

> We're building an open standard for AI-agent commerce — and we're looking for people to tear it apart and build on it.
>
> **Open Agent Commerce** is a small set of open protocols + reference code for how AI agents sign users up, pay, and prove what happened — under signed, revocable consent, with a tamper-evident trail.
>
> MIT / CC BY 4.0. Stewarded neutrally, not owned. Independent — not affiliated with Visa, Google, Stripe or OpenAI; we just interoperate (we ship adapters that normalize Visa TAP, Google AP2 and Stripe/OpenAI ACP into one verifiable request).
>
> It's an early draft (v0.1) and we want it pressure-tested. We're looking for:
> → engineers to port the primitives to another language (conformance vectors included — prove byte-for-byte interop)
> → security folks to break the signing / replay / consent model
> → reviewers to comment on the spec
> → co-maintainers who want to help steer it toward a neutral home
>
> If "AI agents transacting on your behalf" is going to be real, it needs an open, neutral trust layer — built in the open. Come build it.
>
> What would you want to verify before letting an agent spend your money? 👇
>
> #OpenSource #AIagents #AgenticCommerce #Protocols #Fintech

## Post B — invite SaaS companies / startups (acquisition channel)

> AI agents are about to become a user-acquisition channel for SaaS. Most products aren't ready for it.
>
> Soon, when someone tells their AI agent "set me up with a tool for X," the agent will go and sign them up — somewhere. The question for every SaaS founder: will it be *your* product, and on *your* terms?
>
> We built **Open Agent Commerce** — an open protocol that lets an AI agent onboard a user to your product:
> → under the user's signed, revocable consent (they set the limits)
> → with a tamper-evident record of exactly what was authorized
> → and you keep your billing, your rails, your account — no custody, no lock-in
>
> For a SaaS company that's a new top-of-funnel: agents bringing you verified, consenting, paying users — a frictionless channel instead of one more signup form a human abandons.
>
> It's open (MIT / CC BY 4.0) and early (v0.1). We're looking for a handful of SaaS design partners to integrate it and shape where it goes.
>
> Curious what a "sign up via an agent" funnel could look like for your product? DM me, or grab the repo (link in comments).
>
> #SaaS #AIagents #UserAcquisition #Growth #Startups #AgenticCommerce
